To MinGye

Great oaks endure through patient years,
While saplings race then disappear.
Let knowledge seep like morning dew,
True wisdom forms where roots pursue.

In silent study's sacred space,
Where pages turn with measured grace,
Each equation, cold and stark,
Becomes the ember in the dark.

Let others chase the fleeting flame,
You'll build cathedrals from the frame.
For every theorem you command,
New worlds emerge at your command.

The scholar's path winds through the night,
Where stars are proofs and moonlight - insight.
Each failure etched upon the scroll,
Becomes the bedrock of the soul.

Let seasons cycle, spring to fall,
Your mind a forge transforming all.
What seems like dormant winter's sleep,
Is nature's way to harvest deep.

When dawn breaks through your study's veil,
You'll find the cosmos in detail.
For those who master time's slow art,
Eternity dwells in the heart.
